Results

We found out that Q4 is the modal time of year when people spend the most money this can be clearly seen in the median of all categories graph. The only year that does not conform to this is 2008, this is ikely due to the financial crisis during that time. We found that as a rule of thumb consumer spending is proportional to time of year i.e as the year goes on people spending more money. However, this is a rule of thumb from our median graph, if we look at the all categories graph we can see a spike in hotels and restaurants during the third quarter this can be linked to summer holidays. We see a spike in clothing and footwear in Q4 aswell as food and non-alcholic beverages this is due to Christmas holidays during that period. There is always a steep dip in consumer spending in the first quarter of each year across all categories. The constant rise in the line graph displays the year to year inflation in the UK.
