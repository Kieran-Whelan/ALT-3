Background

The dataset that we chose is a UK Consumer Dataset from the years 1997 - 2022. This is a large dataset so we had narrow down what areas of the data we sought to analyse. We decided to only anaylse the data between the years 1997 and 2017 as we believed that 20 years was a sufficient sample period of time. Another reason fro omitting the years from 2018 - 2022 was the COVID-19 pandemic that shock the global economy, subsequently affecting the consumer spending. Our dataset contains the total spending in the UK across all households. The figures are measured in millions. It is comprised by the UK Office for National Statistics and updated yearly.

Hypothesis

Using our chosen dataset, we aim to investigate how time of year affects consumer spending across different categories. We aim to investigate 3 categories in total. As a by-product of our investigation we should be able to observe a steady rise in spending since 1997. This rise can be attributed to many factors with the main one being inflation. The rate of inflation in the UK has been 2.75% since the 1980s our values should be in line with this.
